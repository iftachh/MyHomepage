The is an advance graduate-level course in Cryptography, which continues the course Founatation of Cryptography in this fall .

The exact syllabus will be determined as the semester proceeds, based on the makeup of the class, but roughly we will go as follows:

  • Week 1: ZK,  Review, ZK for DH tuples, Sequential composition of ZK
  • Week 2: Proofs of knowledge, impossibility of  3 round black-box ZK
    Reading: On the Composition of Zero-Knowledge Proof Systems
  • Week 3: Concurrent ZK,  Non-BB ZK
    Reading:
  • Week 4: NIZK + simulation sound NIZK + CCA secure encryption
  • Week 5: Non-malleable Commitment
  • Week 6:  Multi-party computation,  The GMW protocol
    Reading: How to Solve any Multi-Party Protocol Problem
  • Week 7:  The  BGW Protocol
    Reading: Completeness theorems for non-cryptographic fault-tolerant distributed computation
  • Week 8: Basic security, UC security, composition theorem
    Reading: Security and Composition of Cryptographic Protocols: A Tutorial.  
    Universally Composable Security: A New Paradigm for Cryptographic Protocols.
    Security and composition of multi-party cryptographic protocols.
  • Week 9:  Proof of security of BGW, beginning of CLOS
    Reading: Universally composable two-party and multi-party secure computation.
  • Week 10: UC commitments, CLOS
  • Week 11-12:  Parallel repetition of interactive proofs/arguments
    Reading: An Efficient Parallel Repetition Theorem.   A Parallel Repetition Theorem for Any Interactive Argument
  • Week 13-14: Black box separations
    Reading: Finding Collisions in Interactive Protocols -- A Tight Lower Bound on the Round Complexity of Statistically-Hiding Commitments
