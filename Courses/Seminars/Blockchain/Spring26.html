Seminar in Blockchain and Cryptocurrencies
Course id: 0368342401


Iftach Haitner

Tuesdays: 10:00-12:00, Room: Dan David 106


In just over a decade, cryptocurrencies have evolved into a significant force in global finance. In this seminar, we will study the technological aspects of cryptocurrencies, which typically involve combining tools from distributed systems, cryptography, and game theory. For the most part, we will follow the excellent Foundations of Blockchains course by Tim Roughgarden. In addition, we will focus on privacy solutions in cryptocurrencies. In each meeting one student will cover a single topic as listed below.

Prerequisites

Background in the theory of computation (Algorithm, Computability, Complexity) is required. Students with particularly strong math background are also welcome. Cryptography course, like Foundation of Cryptography or Introduction to Modern Cryptography, is recommended but not required.

Presentations

Power Point presentations are acceptable, but white boards ones are preferable.
To do a good job one needs to read background material, see the reading section.
In addition, the speakers of the week will have to give me a practice talk a week before (right after the talk of that week).
